Background & Motivation

Current speech understanding benchmarks focus on single-speaker scenarios or isolated sub-tasks. However, real-world multi-speaker conversations require models to understand who is speaking, what content belongs to whom, and how speakers interact with each other — capabilities that are not systematically evaluated today.

🎯

Beyond Transcription

Existing benchmarks measure transcription quality and speaker attribution error rates, but fail to evaluate whether models truly understand the conversational structure.

🔗

Speaker-Content Binding

A model might correctly transcribe text, yet still fail to determine which speaker said what, how speakers feel, or what their intentions are.

🔄

Cross-Speaker Reasoning

Real conversations require understanding speaker relationships, emotional influence, and dialogue structure — capabilities that demand multi-speaker temporal reasoning.

Two-Tier Task Architecture

MSU-Bench evaluates multi-speaker understanding through two hierarchical tiers: foundational speaker grounding capabilities and advanced dialogue reasoning capabilities.

Tier 1

Speaker Grounding & Identification

Bind speech content, speaker identity, and speaker attributes together — who is who, who said what, whose attribute is this. Two capabilities, ten sub-tasks.

Speaker Identification SID

Ground speaker identities and speaker-specific information across dialogue segments.

Reverse Speaker Retrieval (RSR) Speaker Retrieval (SR) Speaker-specific Viewpoint Summarization (SVS) Speaker Counting (SC) Speaker Verification (SV)
Speaker Attribute Recognition SAR

Infer core speaker attributes and a brief profile from multi-speaker conversational audio.

Accent Identification (AI) Age Recognition (AR) Gender Identification (GI) Emotion Recognition (ER) Speaker Profiling (SP)
Tier 2

Multi-Speaker Dialogue Reasoning

Reason about relationships, structure, and context across speakers — what these utterances mean in the conversation. Three capabilities, six sub-tasks.

Dialogue Scene Reasoning DSR

Infer dialogue background, speaker roles, and situational context from multi-speaker evidence.

Background Inference (BI) Role/Identity Identification (RII)
Dialogue Structure Analysis DSA

Recognize dialogue acts and question–answer relations across turns.

Dialogue Act Recognition (DAR) Q&A Structure ID (QASI)
Dialogue Contextual Reasoning DCR

Reason over multi-speaker context for emotion interactions and viewpoint aggregation.

Emotion Interaction Reasoning (EIR) Multi-speaker Viewpoint Summarization (MSVS)

Five Speaker Referencing Schemes

In real applications, target speakers can be specified in various ways. MSU-Bench evaluates model robustness across five different referencing paradigms.

🎵

No Index

Direct audio snippet as acoustic anchor for speaker identification

⏱️

Time Index

Target speaker specified by timestamp range in the conversation

📝

Transcript Index

Target speaker identified by quoted textual content

🔢

Speaker Index

Target speaker referenced by appearance order

🔗

Complex Index

Combined multiple cues for precise speaker localization

Key Findings & Results

We evaluated 9 representative audio-language models including the Gemini series, Qwen2.5-Omni, Qwen3-Omni, Audio Flamingo 3, Kimi-Audio, Step-Audio 2, and MiMo-Audio. The results reveal important insights about current model capabilities.

Table 1: Overall evaluation results across 9 audio-language models. Gemini-3-Flash achieves the highest overall accuracy.

📊 Key Findings

  • Finding 1: Time Index remains the hardest Models struggle most when speakers must be located by timestamps alone. Gemini-3-Flash achieves only 76% accuracy on Tier 2 Time Index, compared to 92% on Complex Index.
  • Finding 2: Even strong models make wrong-speaker errors While weaker models tend to say "cannot determine", stronger models confidently attribute content to the wrong speaker.
  • Finding 3: General capability ≠ Speaker-centric capability General omni-models do not necessarily outperform specialized audio models. Speaker-centric understanding requires dedicated modeling of speaker identity.

🏆 Performance Comparison

  • Best Overall: Gemini-3-Flash Achieves the highest overall accuracy with 77% overall, demonstrating strong multi-modal reasoning capabilities in conversational scenarios.
  • Best Open-Source: MiMo-Audio Reaches 56% overall accuracy, showing promising open-source alternatives for speaker-centric understanding tasks.
  • Tier Gap: Tier 2 outperforms Tier 1 Most models perform better on Tier 2 reasoning tasks than Tier 1 grounding tasks, suggesting speaker localization remains the bottleneck.
